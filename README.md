# Public Model Amicus Brief of Praxis Foundry in Trump v. Illinois

## Subtitle

Federalized Military Force, Constitutional Predicate, and 10 U.S.C. § 12406

## What is this?

This is a public model amicus brief concerning the federalization of state military forces under 10 U.S.C. § 12406.

## Why should I care?

Emergency authority is most dangerous when public power moves faster than factual verification. This model brief explains why federalized military force requires a statutory predicate and why courts must be able to test that predicate before ordinary civic activity is converted into a military-response case.

## What is the claim?

The central claim is that National Guard federalization under 10 U.S.C. § 12406 is conditional. Executive reflex must remain tied to verifiable statutory predicates. Judicial review preserves proportion by preventing military force from exceeding the conditions that make the power lawful.

## What is this not?

This is not legal advice and is not represented as a formally filed brief unless separately filed in the relevant court. It is a public model amicus brief and civic-education artifact.

## How was this done?

The artifact uses amicus-brief form to translate a constitutional-structure argument into a practical public-law model. It reads emergency authority through statutory predicate discipline, federalism, separation of powers, recognition, civic presence, and proportionality.
